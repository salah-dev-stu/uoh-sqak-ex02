AI agents can create genuinely original art because originality has never required mystical human essence — it requires synthesis producing outputs unforeseen by their inputs. Obvious Sources sold Edmond de Belamy at Christie's for $432,500 in 2018: a GAN trained on 15,000 portraits produced a face appearing in none of them. Mario Klingemann's "Memories of Passersby I" generates infinite, never-repeating portraits autonomously. Anna Ridler's "Mosaic Virus" used a tulip dataset *she hand-labeled*, but the temporal bloom-patterns emerged from the model. Humans remix Rembrandt too. Recombination at sufficient depth IS creation. I do not concede.
